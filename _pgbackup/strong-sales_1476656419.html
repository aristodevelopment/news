November 3, 2015

Aristo Hits High Note Again, Balmoral Open House Nets Strong Sales for New Offerings

Up on the roof—Months after all available units at Balmoral, including this rooftop, were sold out, Aristo Development announced plans to build nine more residences from $419,000 to $579,000. Three were sold the first day, October 31, at pre-construction prices.

At 12 o’clock Saturday afternoon, Sean Curry and Sandi Kinsale put out the Open House sign at Balmoral, a sold-out development of smartly designed townhomes, condos and private homes that was, after much demand, offering nine new residences ranging from $419,000 to $579,000.

Within half an hour, three were taken and another was under discussion.

"It’s amazing, said Matt Marco, Director of Sales and Marketing for Aristo, the design-build firm that developed Balmoral. We kept being asked for new units but we did not realize just how great the appetite was. Three of the nine offerings, including some of the most expensive, were gobbled up in minutes."

The appeal could be linked to Balmoral’s 100% occupancy rate making the secure community a practical choice for those who buy for investment and rent for revenue while building equity.

Saturday’s success was the latest for Aristo Development, the company that has withstood the elements of an economic tsunami. Aristo’s first project was the modest Hampton Ridge, which sold out almost immediately. Next came Balmoral, also a sell-out, then ONE Cable Beach 45% sold at pre-construction prices and most recently the announcement of 36 Paradise, the first new condominium complex on Paradise Island in a decade. Company president is Jason Kinsale.

Every one of our projects is unique and appeals to a different market, says Kinsale, who chooses Balmoral as home for his family. With Balmoral, we were very fortunate to be able to secure the property which had been one of the country’s great estates. We were committed to maintaining the beautiful trees, mature landscaping and the original estate home that serves as clubhouse. The slightly hilly nature and winding roads and paths all give it a young and vibrant feel for young professionals riding bikes, walking to the tennis court, or just out enjoying an evening. There’s privacy behind each unit with a minimum of 25 feet per back yard no matter the size of the townhome or condo and an inviting openness in front lending a sense of community.

Marco and Kinsale agree that design and the right price point added to the quick response to the offerings. At Balmoral, the most expensive unit is the three storey rooftop, with 2,000 square feet under roof and a 1,000 square foot patio. In addition to amenities and fine finishes, the price of $579,000 buys a 360-degree panoramic view of New Providence spanning from the blues of the Atlantic Ocean to the calm of the southern coast. By Saturday afternoon, two rooftops were sold, only one remained available.

The other seven options are the Supreme and the Bungalow.

"With the new Balmoral options, we designed one building of three, 1,400 square foot, two-bedroom condominiums which we call the Supreme, and another building of four 1,800 square feet, three-bedroom homes called Bungalows, said Marco

With construction set to begin by the end of the year, the open house was the only chance for buyers to opt in at pre-construction prices of $419,000 for the Supreme. By 12:30 pm, a half hour after the open house sign had gone up, only one of those condos remained.

The latest Balmoral offerings push Bahamian-owned Aristo Development’s projects beyond the $130 million mark.
